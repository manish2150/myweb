Mission

There’s a belief that video is just about visuals. But I believe it’s about impact, emotion, and results.

My mission is to deliver powerful, cinematic visuals that not only look stunning but also create real value for every client I work with. From creative video editing to professional drone production, I focus on quality, originality, and strategic storytelling that makes brands stand out.

Working with businesses and individuals in and around Pokhara, I aim to create content that feels different — content that captures attention, builds trust, and leaves a lasting impression.

I don’t just create videos — I craft visual experiences that elevate brands, satisfy clients, and set them apart from the competition.

My Story

I’m a 19-year-old student and video editor based in Pokhara. My journey into the digital world started when I was in Class 7. I was deeply curious about how videos were created, how transitions worked, and how simple clips could turn into powerful stories.

At that time, I didn’t have a big goal or business vision — I was just editing for fun. What began as pure curiosity slowly turned into passion. I spent countless hours learning, experimenting, and improving my craft, simply because I loved the process.

Over the years, that passion transformed into purpose. Today, I work with clients in and around Pokhara, creating cinematic edits, drone visuals, and storytelling-driven content that connects emotionally with audiences.

For me, editing is more than just cutting clips — it’s about creating impact. Every project is an opportunity to grow, to innovate, and to deliver results that make my clients proud. What started as fun in Class 7 has now become the foundation of my creative career.

How I Started Editing

It all began in class 7 when I was just a curious kid with a smartphone. I wanted to add a helicopter flying over my school – it seemed like magic. So I downloaded a random editing app and spent hours figuring out how to overlay a tiny helicopter clip. The result? Absolutely hilarious – the helicopter was pixelated, shaky, and way too big. But that feeling of creating something from nothing hooked me forever.

From that funny first experiment, I kept learning, failing, and improving. What started as a childhood curiosity turned into a profession that lets me bring imagination to life. And yes, I still add helicopters sometimes – now they actually look real!
